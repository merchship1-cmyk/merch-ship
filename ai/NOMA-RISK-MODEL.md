# NOMA Risk Model

## Introduction
The NOMA Risk Model is designed to assess the risks associated with Non-Orthogonal Multiple Access (NOMA) systems. It provides a framework for evaluating performance metrics under various conditions.

## Key Components
1. **Channel Models**: Assessing the impact of channel fading and interference.
2. **User Scheduling**: Evaluating the effectiveness of different user scheduling algorithms.
3. **Power Allocation**: Analyzing optimal power allocation strategies for users.

## Risk Factors
- **Interference Levels**: Understanding how interference affects overall system performance.
- **Hardware Limitations**: Considering the impact of real-world hardware constraints.
- **Environmental Factors**: Assessing risks introduced by environmental conditions such as mobility and obstacles.

## Conclusion
The NOMA Risk Model serves as a valuable tool for researchers and practitioners aiming to implement effective NOMA systems while managing associated risks.
